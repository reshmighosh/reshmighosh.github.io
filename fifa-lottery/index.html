The model

  • T_cat = capacity × public-pool fraction × category supply share
  • D_cat = total match demand × category demand share

Calibration & limitations

Expected wins under the median model: —. Actual wins: 1. Getting M5 was the most-likely positive outcome, but the model's expectation across both applications was below 0.1. I got somewhat lucky.

  • Per-match demand dominates uncertainty. M5 sits in the "below 1M" bucket (27 of 104 matches); M97 is firmly above.
  • Category shares are assumed proportional to typical FWC pricing tier splits.
  • Reserved allocations (hospitality, sponsors, federations, media) are folded into one fraction.
  • Downgrade option accepted; slightly increases effective P(win), not modeled.
  • M5–M97 independence holds because my 2-ticket total is far below the 40-ticket cap.
